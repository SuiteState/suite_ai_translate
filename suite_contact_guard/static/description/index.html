Contact Guard

Phone uniqueness, contact field permissions, and customer activity status — for Odoo 19 sales teams

Built for B2B teams where phone numbers are the asset

You run a sales team. Multiple salespeople, one shared contact pool. You want everyone to see that a customer exists (so nobody creates a duplicate), but you don't want one salesperson casually browsing another salesperson's customer phone numbers and emails.

And you want this without breaking quotations, invoices, login, or any other place Odoo touches res.partner behind the scenes.

Three problems Contact Guard solves on day one:

  • Salespeople poaching each other's contacts via casual list browsing and search-as-you-type
  • Duplicate contacts created because nobody noticed the same phone already exists in the system
  • Customers going dark for months with no automatic tracking of who hasn't ordered in a while

Three independent features, configured per company

1. Phone uniqueness

Phone numbers are normalized to digits-only (stripping +, leading 00, spaces, dashes, parentheses) and stored in an indexed field. When a user tries to save a contact with a phone that already exists in the same company, the operation is rejected with an error naming the conflicting number.

Bypassed automatically for system automation, bulk imports (import_file context), and external public/portal users. Configurable minimum length per company (default: 8 digits).

2. Contact field permissions

Once a contact has an assigned salesperson, their phone and email become private to that salesperson and to administrators. Contacts without a salesperson stay visible to all internal users — so anyone can pick up a new lead, but established customers are protected.

The salesperson field itself is locked after creation: only Sales Manager / Contact Guard Admin can reassign customers between salespeople.

Phone edit protection is enforced at the ORM layer, not just the view, so it applies to RPC, scripts, and automated actions in addition to the web UI.

3. Customer activity status

A daily cron computes each customer's activity status from their last confirmed sale order date:

  • Active — recent order, within warning threshold
  • Warning (yellow) — past warning threshold, time to follow up
  • Sleeping (red) — past sleeping threshold, losing them
  • Dormant (muted) — past dormant threshold, probably gone

Status appears on the contact form, on the contact list with colour decoration, and is available for filtering and grouping. Thresholds are configurable per company.

Why this works without breaking your sales workflow

Most "private contacts" modules on the Apps Store and most forum answers tell you to add a record rule on res.partner like [('user_id', '=', user.id)]. We tried that route first and abandoned it.

res.partner is the central reference hub of Odoo. The moment you tighten read access on it with a record rule, every place that implicitly loads a related partner can fail with AccessError:

  • Salespeople cannot open their own quotations (partner_invoice_id / partner_shipping_id access denied)
  • Login fails because res.users reverse-references the user's own partner record
  • Chatter, followers, activities throw "Implicitly accessed through Users" errors
  • Multi-company partners (companion partners of res.company) become invisible and break warehouse and journal routing

Existing solutions to these errors are all unpleasant: edit the built-in private-employee record rule, split internal users into two parallel groups, add a chain of OR clauses for every new edge case. Every workaround leaks. Odoo itself removed the original res.partner.rule.private rule in version 17 because the design caused too many regressions.

Contact Guard takes a different stance. Contact records stay fully visible to all internal users — quotations open, chatter loads, login works. Phone, email, and salesperson are the things you actually want to protect, and those are gated through field-level groups, ORM write hooks, and view-level groups.

Configuration

Contact Guard configures per company under Contacts -> Configuration -> Contact Guard (visible to System Administrators).

Important: the protection features are on by default and do not require a configuration record. Phone uniqueness, field permissions, and salesperson lock start working the moment you install the module. Configuration only lets you tune parameters (such as the minimum phone length) or opt in to the activity tracking cron.

Feature Default state What configuration changes
Phone uniqueness On Adjusts the minimum digit count (default 8)
Field permissions (phone / email / salesperson) On No configuration; behaviour is fixed by role
Customer activity status Off Enables the daily cron and sets Warning / Sleeping / Dormant thresholds

Compatibility

  • Odoo 19.0 (Community and Enterprise)
  • Multi-company aware: every check and configuration is scoped per company
  • No database-level constraints; enforcement at the ORM, field, or view layer depending on the rule
  • Depends only on standard Odoo modules: contacts, mail, sales_team, sale, purchase
